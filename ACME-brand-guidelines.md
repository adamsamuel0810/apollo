This document codifies the detailed presentation standards observed consistently across the provided PowerPoint decks. It is intended to function as a practical, enforceable style guide for all future presentations.

# Requirements by PowerPoint Element

## Title Slides

- Typeface: Calibri (Headings)
- Size: 24 pt
- Valid date is noted. No "DRAFT" or other designator
- Authors are identified

## Section Header Slides

- Typeface: Calibri (Headings)
- Size: 24 pt

## Content Slides

ACME's slides contain one or more combinations of:

- Bulleted lists of text
- Tables of data
- Charts and Visuals
- Collections of PowerPoint shapes

### Overall

- Clear separation between slide title and body content

### Slide Title (Headline)

- Typeface: Calibri Bold
- Size: 24 pt
- Case: Sentence case
- Punctuation: No ending punctuation
- Placement: Vertically centered
- Size: Same as "Title and Content" master slide
- Position: Same as "Title and Content" master slide
- Content rule: Titles communicate the takeaway, not just the topic
- Length: Flag/warn if headline exceeds 3 lines

### Confidentiality Statement

- Font: Calibri (Body)
- Size: 8 pt
- Position: Same as "Title and Content" master slide
- Legal language is not modified or paraphrased
- Appears on every slide, **including title and section header slides**

## Content Slide Content

### Overall

- Warn if any content is closer to the left edge of the slide than the left edge of the Slide Title
- Warn if any content is closer to the right edge of the slide than the right edge of the Slide Title
- No element below the header should be less than 1" from the left edge of the slide

### Bulleted Lists

- Typeface: Calibri (Body)
- Size of primary bullets: 16 pt
- Size of sub-bullets: 16 pt
- Bullet symbols:
  - Level 1: square bullets — accepts ∎ (Alt-254) or the ▪ variant (both treated as equivalent)
  - Level 2: dashes or en-dashes (--)
  - Level 3: three-D Top-Lighted Rightwards Arrowhead (Alt-10146)
  - Levels 4–5: black circles
- Alignment & indentation: Bullets are left-aligned with consistent indentation across slides
- Line spacing: Single line spacing within bullet text
- Spacing between Level 1 bullets: 6 pt
- Spacing between Level 2, level 3 and level 4-5 bullets: 0 pt
- Indention of Level 2 bullets: .4"
- Indention of Level 3, level 4-5 bullets: .6"
- Punctuation: Bullet text does not end in punctuation
- Writing mechanics: Parallel grammatical structure within a list

### Tables

#### Overall

- Typeface: Calibri
- Size: 10–12 pt. Consistent across the table
- Borders: No left or right border on the table
- Visual design
  - Gridlines are subtle or removed
  - Tables favor clarity over density
  - Avoid unnecessary shading or color
- Number formatting:
  - Consistent decimal precision within a table
  - Percentages always shown with the % symbol
  - Currency values always shown with a currency symbol
  - Use en-dashes or blanks for not-applicable or missing values
- Percentiles are labeled with "%ile", not "Percentile"

#### Table Title

- Typeface: Calibri (Body) Bold
- Size: 14 pt
- Spacing between lines: 0 pt
- Position: Centered above table

#### Table Header

- Typeface: Bold
- Alignment: Bottom aligned
- Font color: White
- Shading: #006EBE
- Borders: Thin black line on top and bottom. No vertical borders at left and right
- Logically grouped columns: white lines within the header row can be used to logically group columns and rows. A thin column will be between the column groups to create visual separation between the groups
- Structure: Could be more than one line
- Header terminology for employee tables
  - Use "Executive" (not "Incumbent") for the column containing employee names
  - Use "Title" or "Position" for the column containing job titles
- *Informative, not enforced.* Unless units are noted in the header for each column, a unit's designator may be required in the upper left corner of the header. Particularly for tables containing compensation figures. It generally takes the form ($000s) and is displayed in italics. **TODO:** candidate new rule — not built yet.
- No left or right border on the table

#### Table Body

- Shading: Zebra striping with White Background 1 (#F2F2F2). First row is "no fill" and then alternating with #F2F2F2
- Justification: Consistent within each column

#### Statistics Row(s)

- The Statistics Row can be identified as containing summary statistics of the data above, including 25th %ile, median, 75th %ile
- The statistics row may not be present below a table
- Borders: Thin black line on top and bottom. No vertical borders at left and right
- Shading: Yellow, Accent 3 (#FFFFDB)
- *Informative, not enforced.* Stats row(s) are above the client row(s) ~90% of the time

#### Client Row(s)

- The Client Row can be identified as containing the name of the client
- The Client Row may not be present
- Borders: Thin black line on top and bottom. No vertical borders at left and right
- Shading: Light Green, Accent 2 (#F1FFE1)

### Charts and Visuals

#### Chart Title

- Typeface: Calibri (Body) Bold
- Size: 14 pt
- Spacing between lines: 0 pt
- Position: Centered above the chart or other visual

#### Content

- Content: Picture from Excel or some other tool (not PowerPoint elements)
- Axis labels and legends are clearly labeled
- Color palette: All colors are from the ACME color palette
- Tables should not be pasted as Charts and Visuals but as PowerPoint native tables

### Collections of PowerPoint Shapes

- Typeface: Calibri (Body)
- Size: Consistent across all shapes
- Colors: Drawn from ACME Color Palette

### Footnotes & Sources

- Typeface: Calibri (Body) Italicized
- Size: 10 pt
- All footnote markers (e.g., superscripts) used within slide text, tables, or graphics must have a matching footnote entry at the bottom of the same slide; orphaned/unmatched references are not permitted

### Chart Source Notes

- Typeface: Calibri (Body) Italicized
- Size: 9 pt
- Placement: Directly below the chart or visual

> Chart source notes are a distinct element from general slide footnotes. Slide footnotes stay at 10 pt; chart source notes are 9 pt.

### Emphasis and Annotations

- Red boxes are used to attract attention to specific information. Information in the box should also be referenced in the headline of the slide

## Writing Style & Terminology

- Board-ready, professional tone
- Declarative, insight-driven headlines
- Minimal jargon; use established compensation terminology
- Consistent terminology across slides and decks
- Focus on implications, not narration

## Other

- Spell out "Target" (do not abbreviate as "TGT")

# Appendix A: Do / Don't Examples for Presentation Standards

This appendix provides concrete examples to illustrate correct application of the presentation standards. Examples are described structurally so they can be recreated directly in PowerPoint.

## Slide Title Example

| DO | DON'T |
|---|---|
| Title: "FY26–28 LRSU goal ranges have been modestly tightened to reflect revenue mix" • Calibri Bold, 24 pt • Bottom-aligned title box • No punctuation • Title states the takeaway | Title: "LRSU Plan Design" • Topic-only title • Center-aligned • Ends with punctuation • Requires narration to understand meaning |

## Bullet Structure Example

| DO | DON'T |
|---|---|
| • Goal ranges were narrowed to reflect slower movement in recurring revenue -- Recurring revenue now represents a larger share of total revenue -- Reduces volatility and external noise • Two bullet levels maximum • Parallel grammar | • Goal ranges were narrowed. • Recurring revenue is now a larger part of revenue mix which means it moves slower and therefore this change was made. • Multiple sentence styles • Overly dense text |

## Table Formatting Example

| DO | DON'T |
|---|---|
| Header row in Bold. Metric \| Threshold \| Target \| Max. Organic Revenue Growth \| 6.0% \| 8.5–10.0% \| >11.5% • Numeric columns right-aligned • No shading • Consistent % formatting | Heavy gridlines. Shaded header row. Mixed decimal precision. Centered numeric values. |

## Color Usage Example

| DO | DON'T |
|---|---|
| • Black body text • Blue accent used for key data point • Yellow highlight used only to indicate changed figures | Multiple accent colors. Decorative color usage. Red text used for non-risk content. Yellow highlight used for emphasis only. |

## Chart Design Example

| DO | DON'T |
|---|---|
| • Left-aligned title above chart • Blue primary series, gray secondary series • Minimal gridlines • Source noted below chart in 9 pt font | Centered chart title. Multiple bright colors. 3D effects. No data source. |

## Emphasis & Annotation Example

| DO | DON'T |
|---|---|
| Bold used to highlight key conclusion. Strikethrough only to show prior targets. Yellow highlight only for committee decision points. | Underlining entire paragraphs. Italics for emphasis. Multiple highlight colors. |

## Spacing & Density Example

| DO | DON'T |
|---|---|
| • Adequate white space • ~6 pt spacing between bullets • Clear separation between title and body | Overcrowded slide. Minimal spacing between bullets. Content pushed to slide edges. |
